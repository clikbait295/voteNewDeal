Federal Security Administration (FSA)

The Federal Security Agency (FSA) is an independent agency that regulates various subdivision agencies. Formed by the Reorganization Act of 1939, the FSA strives to bring the federal programs regarding health, education, and social security into one complete sphere. As such, the agency oversees the safety of food and drugs, public health program outreaches, and education funding. Those in need of government assistance are suggested to contact the FSA, who will happily place you into one of the many relief programs it manages.
