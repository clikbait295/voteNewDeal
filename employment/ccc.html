Unemployment Help

Civilian Conservation Corps

While in office, Mr. Roosevelt understood that hundreds of thousands, if not millions of America’s best and brightest boys suffered from unemployment during the Depression. Which is why he, alongside Robert Fechner of the International Association of Machinists, created the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC), an agency which seeks to give little Timmy and cousin Jack a place in America’s workforce! The CCC employs our young men to maintain the evergreen forests, and conserve the wide pasture lands covering this great country, and from building bridges, to benches, the agency also understands that the rough natural resources of America must be refined, so they may give prosperity for our citizens in the future. The CCC’s Indian Division employs over 15,000 native tribesmen, and teaches them the skills needed to survive in the new world. From radio operators to technicians, every Indian in the CCC will leave knowing that they have the know-how to provide for themselves, and their tribe.
